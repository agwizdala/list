10 Important Things from The National Museum of Computing

  • Colossus: The first programmable digital computer, used for code-breaking during WWII.
  • The Bombe: Device used by British codebreakers to decrypt Enigma-encrypted messages during WWII.
  • WITCH: Recognized as the world's oldest working digital computer.
  • EDSAC Replica: A rebuild of the Electronic Delay Storage Automatic Calculator, an early British computer.
  • Harwell Dekatron: An early relay-based computer.
  • Elliot 803: Represents the second generation of computers with its transistors.
  • IBM System 360: A mainframe computer from the 1960s that was widely adopted in industry.
  • Tunny & Heath Robinson: Machines related to the code-breaking efforts during WWII.
  • Personal Computer Gallery: Exhibiting the evolution of personal computers from the 1970s onwards.
  • Micro Gallery: Focused on microprocessors and their impact on computing.
